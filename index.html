AWS Certified Cloud Practitioner — Enhanced 14-Day Two-Slot Study Plan

Two daily slots: Morning (phone/tablet — reading & spaced-repetition) and Evening (laptop — labs, demos, practice). Progress saved locally (localStorage). This enhanced plan adds study strategy, tracking, and test-day checklist.

Is this plan realistic?

Short answer: Yes, realistic for an experienced 15+ year developer, provided disciplined focus, daily practice questions, and at least 2–3 full timed mocks (65-question equivalent) before booking. The plan assumes ~3–5 hours/day split into two ≤2.5-hr slots. The candidate's software background maps well to exam concepts — the exam rewards breadth and question-reading skill more than deep infra experience.

Key risks & mitigations

  • Risk: Rushing hands-on labs — mitigation: focus on small, repeatable console tasks (S3, EC2, Lambda, IAM) and delete resources immediately.
  • Risk: Overreliance on one practice bank — mitigation: use at least 2-3 different practice sources to learn varied question wording.
  • Risk: Burnout — mitigation: take short breaks, keep mornings lighter (flashcards, 20–30 mins), reserve evenings for practice/labs.

What I added to the plan

  • Daily spaced repetition / Anki-style flashcard step each morning (15–25 min).
  • Explicit error log task: after every practice set, log wrong answers and classify by concept (IAM, pricing, storage, networking, wording trap).
  • Minimum of 3 full timed practice exams (Days 11,12,13 slots adjusted) from different providers or mixes of official + community sets.
  • Exam-day & online proctoring checklist (ID, environment, system check, timers, snacks).
  • Test-taking strategies: time management, elimination, when to mark+skip, reading for qualifiers (NOT, EXCEPT), and how to use the review feature.
  • Contingency: immediate 3-day remediation plan if mock scores < 70% at Day 12.

Time allocation by domain (approx.)

Allocate study time roughly proportional to exam weights but also account for personal weaknesses:

Cloud Concepts: 20% | Security: 25% | Technology/Core services: 30% | Billing & Pricing: 25%

Over 42 total study hours, that maps roughly to: Concepts 8–10 hrs, Security 10–12 hrs, Technology 12–14 hrs, Billing 10–12 hrs (distributed across 14 days).

Study strategy & tracking templates

Daily Error Log (recommended format)

{"date":"YYYY-MM-DD","question_id_or_source":"ExamPro #12","your_answer":"B","correct":"C","topic":"IAM","notes":"Confused role vs user; re-read IAM roles doc"}

Keep this as a plain text / Google Sheet / local file. After each practice set, add wrong answers and review them on the following morning's flashcard session.

Feynman quick-check (5 min)

At the end of each evening session, pick the weakest topic and explain it out loud in simple terms as if teaching someone who knows nothing about cloud. If you can't, make a quick note and revisit tomorrow.

Test-day & proctor checklist

  • ID: Government ID matching your AWS Cert account name (passport or driver's license).
  • Environment: Quiet, well-lit room, clean desk, no other people. Clear the desk of papers and devices unless allowed.
  • System check: Do the Pearson VUE/PSI system check (camera, mic, browser) at least 24 hrs before.
  • Timing: 90 minutes for 65 questions → ~1.38 mins/question. Aim to mark+skip hard questions and return if time.
  • During exam: Read entire stem, note absolute qualifiers (always, never), watch for "EXCEPT"/"NOT". Use elimination.
  • Logistics: Have water and a light snack ready; schedule exam when you are normally most alert (mid-morning or early afternoon recommended).

If you're below target at Day 12 (score <70%) — 3-day remediation plan

  1. Day A: Re-do core Skill Builder modules for your weakest domain + 50 targeted practice Qs on that domain.
  2. Day B: Hands-on mini-labs for misunderstood services (S3 lifecycle, IAM role demo, VPC quick inspect) + 50 mixed practice Qs.
  3. Day C: Full timed mock + review error log; if score still low, postpone exam by 7–10 days and repeat focused cycle.
